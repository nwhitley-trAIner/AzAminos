# 03 — Visual Style Analysis

> Dominant aesthetic tropes across the peptide / research chemical vertical. Color palettes, typography, iconography, photography, logo patterns. Grouped into archetypes with positioning recommendation.

---

## Style Archetypes

After analyzing 12+ competitor sites, the visual landscape clusters into four archetypes:

### Archetype 1: Clinical White / Lab-Grade (RECOMMENDED)

**Sites:** Limitless Biotech, Direct Peptides, Particle Peptides, Biotech Peptides, Cernum Biosciences

**Characteristics:**
- **Palette:** White/off-white backgrounds, muted blues (#36aadd range), soft grays, occasional teal/green accents
- **Typography:** Clean sans-serif (Inter, Roboto, system fonts). Technical, high-legibility. Generous line-height.
- **Photography:** Product vials on white backgrounds, molecular renders, clean lab imagery
- **Iconography:** Simple line icons for trust badges (shield, checkmark, flask, USA flag)
- **Layout:** Generous whitespace, restrained color usage, monochrome backdrops
- **Feel:** "You're ordering from a lab supply company, not a supplement store"

Per [YourPeptideBrand](https://yourpeptidebrand.com/the_power_of_minimalism_in_peptide_brand_design/):
> "Minimalist design using generous whitespace and a restrained color palette of whites, muted blues, and soft grays conveys clarity that mirrors the rigor of lab work behind peptide products."

> "Minimalist packaging that strips away unnecessary graphics and uses monochrome backdrops conveys confidence and research-grade quality, with a single accent line reassuring physicians and clinic staff of rigorous standards."

**Why this wins for premium positioning:** Communicates scientific credibility, quality, and transparency. Distances from supplement/bodybuilding aesthetics. Aligns with the trust-forward positioning needed for payment processor compliance (sites that look "professional" and "scientific" get approved faster per VERIFIED's guidance).

### Archetype 2: Dark/Neon Performance (Supplement-Adjacent)

**Sites:** Swiss Chems (#0a0b0b dark background), Sports Technology Labs, Element SARMs, Peptide Pros

**Characteristics:**
- **Palette:** Dark backgrounds (#0a0b0b, #1a1a2e), neon accents (green, orange, electric blue), high contrast
- **Typography:** Bold, condensed headings. Impact-style or geometric sans-serif.
- **Photography:** Lifestyle/fitness imagery mixed with product shots, sometimes molecular renders on dark backgrounds
- **Iconography:** Badges and shields with glow effects, cryptocurrency logos prominently displayed
- **Layout:** Dense product grids, promotional banners, "sale" badges, urgency cues
- **Feel:** "Performance supplement store that happens to sell research chemicals"

**Trade-offs:** Higher conversion for the bodybuilding/fitness audience, but may trigger compliance concerns with payment processors. Looks less "research-legitimate." Chemyo uses a dark footer (#0a0b0b) with light text (#bdbdc7) but keeps the main body cleaner.

### Archetype 3: WordPress Default / No-Design

**Sites:** Core Peptides (Divi theme), Loti Labs (WooCommerce default), various smaller vendors

**Characteristics:**
- **Palette:** Whatever the WordPress theme shipped with — usually blues and whites but without intentional design
- **Typography:** Theme defaults (often Open Sans or Lato)
- **Photography:** Inconsistent — some product images, some stock photos, some none
- **Layout:** Standard WooCommerce grid, sidebar filters, default widgets
- **Feel:** "Someone set up a store and focused on product, not design"

**Trade-offs:** These sites often have loyal customers who care about product quality over aesthetics. But first-time visitors may bounce due to lack of trust signals. Won't win on first impressions.

### Archetype 4: Crypto-Native / Privacy-Forward

**Sites:** Next Era Peptide

**Characteristics:**
- **Palette:** Dark with accent colors, minimal chrome
- **Typography:** Modern sans-serif, technically oriented
- **Photography:** Minimal — compound names and dosages take center stage over imagery
- **Iconography:** Crypto logos (BTC, ETH, USDC, USDT) prominently displayed
- **Layout:** Stripped down, functional, almost terminal-aesthetic
- **Feel:** "Privacy-first, no-bullshit compound supplier"

**Hero copy sets the tone:**
> "Serious compounds. Crypto checkout. No friction."
> "No banks, no card processors, no middlemen."

**Trade-offs:** Appeals to privacy-conscious buyers and crypto-native audience. May alienate mainstream buyers who want a traditional shopping experience.

## Typography Patterns

| Pattern | Sites | Font Families | Notes |
|---------|-------|--------------|-------|
| **Clean Technical** | Limitless, Direct, Particle | Inter, Roboto, Nunito Sans | High legibility, professional |
| **Bold Performance** | STL, Swiss Chems, Element | Oswald, Montserrat Bold, Raleway | Impact-driven, urgency |
| **Theme Default** | Core, Loti, Biotech | Open Sans, Lato, system | Functional but generic |

Per [GL Peptides brand design case study](https://markaworks.com/portfolio/gl-peptides/):
> "Typography uses a clean, technical approach engineered for legibility, trust, and a modern pharmaceutical aesthetic."

Per [Dribbble peptide ecommerce concept](https://dribbble.com/shots/26081234-Peptide-Ecommerce-Website-Clean-Modern-UI-Design):
> The dominant aesthetic on design platforms is "Clean & Modern UI Design" — white space, blue accents, card-based layouts.

## Color Palette Patterns

### Clinical / Trust-Forward
```
Primary:    #FFFFFF (white)
Secondary:  #F5F7FA (light gray)
Accent:     #2563EB (trust blue) or #0EA5E9 (sky blue)
Text:       #1F2937 (dark gray, not pure black)
Success:    #10B981 (green for purity/verified badges)
```

### Dark / Performance
```
Primary:    #0A0B0B (near-black)
Secondary:  #1A1A2E (dark navy)
Accent:     #00FF88 (neon green) or #F97316 (orange)
Text:       #BDBDC7 (muted light gray)
CTA:        #EF4444 (red for urgency) or #3B82F6 (blue for trust)
```

### Pharmaceutical / Premium
```
Primary:    #FFFFFF (white)
Secondary:  #E5E7EB (border gray)
Accent:     #1D4ED8 (deep blue)
Text:       #111827 (near-black)
Badge:      #059669 (emerald for verified)
Subtle:     #6B7280 (gray for metadata like CAS#, mol weight)
```

## Photography & Imagery Patterns

| Style | Frequency | Examples |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Vial on white** | Most common (70%) | Clean product photography of lyophilized vials with labels |
| **Molecular render** | Moderate (40%) | 3D molecular structure renders, often on dark backgrounds |
| **Lab equipment** | Low-moderate (30%) | HPLC machines, lab benches, test tubes — signals legitimacy |
| **Lifestyle/fitness** | Low (20%) | Only on SARMs-focused sites — never on premium peptide sites |
| **Abstract science** | Low (15%) | DNA helixes, neural network imagery, cellular microscopy |
| **No imagery** | Rare (10%) | Text-only product listings (Next Era Peptide) |

**Key insight:** The more "clinical" a site's photography, the more premium it's perceived. Lifestyle/fitness imagery correlates with SARMs-heavy catalogs and lower trust scores. Premium peptide vendors avoid human imagery entirely.

## Logo Patterns

| Pattern | Examples | Description |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| **Wordmark + scientific element** | Limitless Biotech, Core Peptides | Company name with a molecular/hexagonal icon |
| **Abbreviation/monogram** | STL, Swiss Chems | Shortened name, often in a shield or circle |
| **Clean wordmark only** | Direct Peptides, Particle Peptides | Just the name, sans-serif, no icon |
| **Mascot/character** | Rare (Peptide Pros has a stylized "PP") | Almost never used in this vertical |

**Brand naming patterns:**
- Compound/science reference: "Peptide Sciences," "Biotech Peptides," "Particle Peptides"
- Lab/research reference: "Loti Labs," "Sports Technology Labs," "Direct Peptides"
- Aspirational: "Limitless," "Ascension," "Next Era," "Pure Rawz"
- Edgy/fitness: "Swole AF," "Pumping Iron," "Element SARMs"

## Recommendation for AzAminos

**Archetype: Clinical White / Lab-Grade** (Archetype 1) — with selective elements from Archetype 4 (crypto-native).

### Reasoning:

1. **Trust-forward positioning** is the primary differentiator in a market where vendor churn is high and many sites look unprofessional
2. **Payment processor compliance** — VERIFIED and other high-risk processors explicitly prefer sites that look "professional" and "scientific" over supplement-store aesthetics
3. **Premium pricing support** — clean, clinical design justifies premium pricing vs. budget competitors
4. **Crypto integration** should feel native (like Next Era Peptide) not bolted-on — give crypto payment the same visual weight as credit cards

### Suggested Palette:
```
Background:     #FFFFFF / #F8FAFC
Surface:        #F1F5F9
Border:         #E2E8F0
Primary:        #1E40AF (deep blue — trust, science)
Secondary:      #0EA5E9 (sky blue — lighter accent)
Success:        #059669 (emerald — verified, purity badges)
Text Primary:   #0F172A
Text Secondary: #64748B
CTA:            #2563EB (blue button, not red/orange urgency)
```

### Design Principles:
- White space > density
- Product vials on white > lifestyle photography
- COA and purity badges in the hero, not buried in footer
- Crypto logos at same visual hierarchy as credit card logos
- Mobile-first responsive
- No "sale" badges, countdown timers, or urgency dark patterns
